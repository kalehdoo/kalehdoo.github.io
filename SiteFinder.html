Kalehdoo Clinical Site Finder

Made Clinical Trials search easy, intuitive, and interactive

If you are looking for a new treatment for yourself or your patients, or want to know where are the clinical trials recruiting on the globe then Kalehdoo Site Finder can help you.

What are Clinical Trials?

A clinical trial is a research conducted with patients to evaluate a new medical treatment or drug/medicine. The purpose of clinical trials is to find new and improved methods of treating, preventing, screening for, and diagnosing different diseases. Clinical Trials helps in bringing new drugs applying latest scientific and technological advances. Every Clinical Trial needs patients who are willing to participate and try investigative new drugs or therapies.

Why should I participate in a clinical Trial?
  • Get early access to the new treatment at reduced costs.
  • Help researchers develop new drugs faster for chronic and rare diseases.
  • Entire community benefits from new drugs made available to patients suffering.
How to use Kalehdoo Clinical Site Finder?

Kalehdoo Clinical Site Finder is an easy to use and interactive application. You can start using the application in 3 simple steps. The application is responsive to all screens so it will adjust automatically to desktops, laptops, tablets and mobile phones. Since the application has data intensive, it may take a few seconds to load initially.

Step 1: Register/Login

To prevent any misuse of the appication, you will be presented with Register or Login Screen. You can choose to authenticate using your Google, Facebook, or any email account.

For the first time, you will be prompted to set up your password, and your account will be registered. If you are registered with an email account, launch on the application URL again and login using your credentials you just setup.

Step 2: Search Criteria

After you login, you will land on Globe tab where you can enter your search criteria. The disease is defaulted to COVID-19 but you can change it to any disease. The search is case-insensitive, so you can type in lower or upper case.

Once you are done with the search criteria, hit the orange button (Display Results). Wait for a few seconds and the results would appear on the map right below the button. Zoom in or out using the plus/minus icons.

Step 3: Select Site

The numbers show clinical sites available in that region. Hover over the mouse over the circles and the region covered will get highlighted in blue. Click on the circles to drill down until you find the clinical site closer to your desired location.

The blue location icon represents a clinical site. Click on it once to view the details on a popup. Click on blue colored Study ID to view detailed description and contact details on clinicaltrials.gov in a new tab.
